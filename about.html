Katerina Magkaniari (Magarini) embodies the creative fusion of artistry, design, and technology. With a foundation in architecture, she explored the synergy between physical structures and digital spaces. Intrigued by the vast possibilities of the digital realm, she delved into web development, experimenting with the art of crafting immersive online experiences. Her enthusiasm for continuous learning and artistic exploration led her to pursue a Master's degree in Digital Arts at the Athens School of Fine Arts, where she delved deep into the intricacies of the digital medium.

With a profound passion for symbiosis, human/non-human interaction, and the exploration of meaning within interconnected spaces, her work delves into the intersection of physical, public, digital, and personal realms, embracing a posthuman perspective. Magarini's art serves as a bridge between the tangible and the intangible, inviting viewers to reconsider their relationships with space, technology, and the complexities of the human condition.

Her creations serve as bridges, linking individuals through shared experiences and collective meaning, embracing the nuances of embodied sensations, the poetics of the everyday and the transformative power of genuine connections.

Her work, characterized by collaborative and playful elements, has been featured in exhibitions and publications, including the Athens Digital Arts Festival, Primarolia Festival, School of Commons, as well as installations in public spaces in collaboration with Goethe Institute and OTE Museum.
